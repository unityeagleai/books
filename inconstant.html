a novel that obeys the moon

Inconstant

You are reading under a borrowed moon. Tonight's is the ringed one below.

One night, July, the lake, the year we were seventeen. I have been telling it to myself for forty years and the telling obeys the moon. I have checked. I keep records. What follows is tonight's version, and it is not the same book you would be holding next week, and neither version is lying, exactly. The moon does not lie. It just never shows you all of anything at once.
